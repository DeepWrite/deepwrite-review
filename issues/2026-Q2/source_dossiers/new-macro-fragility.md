# Source Dossier: The New Macro Fragility

## Core Question

Is the world economy merely slowing, or has it entered a new pattern of repeated resilience under increasingly fragile conditions?

## Provisional Thesis

The 2026 macro picture suggests a fragile resilience: growth continues, but it depends on assumptions about limited conflict, manageable energy prices, trade adaptation, and technology-related investment that are individually plausible and collectively brittle.

## Strongest Opposing Thesis

The world economy has already adapted to shocks better than expected; the language of fragility may understate institutional learning and private-sector flexibility.

## Primary Sources Needed

- IMF WEO April 2026.
- OECD Economic Outlook June 2026.
- World Bank Global Economic Prospects 2026.
- Central bank statements and inflation data.
- IEA energy data.
- WTO trade data.

## Relevant Statistics

- Global, US, euro area, China, and Korea GDP forecasts.
- Inflation forecasts.
- Energy price assumptions.
- Trade growth forecasts.
- Current account and investment indicators where relevant.

## Evidence Strength

Forecast evidence is moderate, not high. It is model-based, scenario-dependent, and vulnerable to new shocks.

## Risks Of Overstatement

- Turning forecasts into facts.
- Treating energy shocks as permanent without scenario evidence.
- Overlooking places where growth remains robust.
