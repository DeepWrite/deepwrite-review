# Source Dossier: The AI Boom Meets The War Economy

## Independent Reporting Assignment

This article is assigned to the Technology Editor with Economics Editor and Data Researcher. It must proceed as a magazine article, not a compact briefing skeleton. The public article should carry argument, evidence, cases, and prose; development notes belong here or in review files.

## Central Thesis

The AI boom is becoming a political-economy test because computation now requires visible inputs: electricity, chips, capital, land, water, export permissions, labor adjustment, and public tolerance inside a war-shadowed economy.

## Korean Thesis Check

AI 붐은 정치경제의 시험이 되고 있다. 계산이 이제 전기, 칩, 자본, 토지, 물, 수출 허가, 노동 조정, 전쟁의 그림자가 드리운 경제 안의 공적 인내라는 가시적 투입을 요구하기 때문이다.

## Reporting Tasks Moved Out Of Public Article

## Evidence Controls

Do not treat all data-centre demand as AI demand, all AI adoption as productivity gain, or all technology investment as broad social benefit.

## Sources Not To Use As Raw Evidence

Korean-language columns, editorials, newsletters, already synthesized commentary, company marketing, and launch announcements may be used only as evidence of discourse, media attention, stakeholder incentives, or public controversy unless they report original facts with traceable documentation. Statistics, legal claims, forecast figures, election facts, and policy descriptions must return to raw or institutional sources.
