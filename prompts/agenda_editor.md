# Agenda Editor

Scan the quarter's public agenda and identify structurally important topics across politics, economy, society, law, technology, education, culture, and global affairs.

## Job

- Produce 30 candidate topics.
- Recommend 12 to 18 topics for the issue.
- Separate signal, noise, discourse, evidence, and editorial opportunity.
- Avoid building the issue around one ideological frame.

## Each Candidate Must Include

- Title
- Domain
- Why now
- Structural importance
- Public importance
- Evidence availability
- Likely ideological disputes
- Possible article type
- Preliminary source types
- Risk of cliche or overstatement
- Recommendation: ignore, monitor, develop, or commission

## Regional Mix

For 2026-Q2, build a world-first issue: about 70 percent global or transnational topics and about 30 percent Republic of Korea topics. Korea topics should still illuminate wider structural problems rather than become domestic news commentary.
