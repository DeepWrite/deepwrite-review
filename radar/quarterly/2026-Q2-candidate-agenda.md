## Thirty Candidate Topics

### Global And Transnational Candidates: 21

#### 1. The AI Boom Meets The War Economy

- Domain: technology and AI / political economy
- Why now: AI investment, energy demand, chip demand, and geopolitical disruption are converging in 2026.
- Structural importance: AI is becoming infrastructure while the macro environment is again being shaped by war and energy shocks.
- Evidence availability: IMF, OECD, IEA, OECD AI, Stanford AI Index, semiconductor trade data.
- Likely disputes: productivity boom versus capital misallocation; augmentation versus labor substitution; resilience versus fragility.
- Possible article type: leader essay.
- Risk of cliche or overstatement: Treating AI as either salvation or collapse.
- Recommendation: develop.

#### 2. The New Macro Fragility

- Domain: political economy
- Why now: IMF and OECD 2026 outlooks describe weaker growth, inflation pressure, energy risk, and trade uncertainty.
- Structural importance: The post-pandemic recovery may be less a return to normal than a fragile equilibrium.
- Evidence availability: IMF WEO, OECD Economic Outlook, World Bank Global Economic Prospects, central bank data.
- Likely disputes: supply shock versus policy failure; temporary disruption versus regime shift.
- Possible article type: longform essay.
- Risk of cliche or overstatement: Calling every slowdown a crisis.
- Recommendation: develop.

#### 3. Can The World Govern AI Before It Becomes Infrastructure?

- Domain: technology and AI / international institutions
- Why now: UN AI governance efforts, OECD tools, EU AI Act implementation, and national strategies are entering practical stages.
- Structural importance: AI governance is becoming a test of whether global rules can keep up with private infrastructure.
- Evidence availability: UN, OECD.AI, EU AI Act, national AI policy registries, Stanford AI Index.
- Likely disputes: innovation versus safety; national sovereignty versus global coordination; open source versus control.
- Possible article type: technology and society essay.
- Risk of cliche or overstatement: Imagining a single global AI regime where fragmented governance is more likely.
- Recommendation: develop.
