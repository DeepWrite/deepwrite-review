# Evidence Policy

Evidence grading is mandatory for major articles.

## Evidence Levels

- `high`: Primary source, official data, peer-reviewed finding, or directly verifiable legal/institutional record.
- `moderate`: Credible secondary synthesis with transparent sourcing.
- `contested`: Credible sources disagree, methods are disputed, or inference is uncertain.
- `low`: Thin evidence, preliminary findings, weak measurement, or unverified reporting.
- `discourse_only`: Used only to show what people are saying, not what is true.

## Statistical Claims

Every statistical claim must include source, date, unit, geography, denominator, method, limitations, and whether comparisons are valid.

## Causal Claims

Causal claims require explicit mechanism, comparison, counterfactual, and alternative explanations.
