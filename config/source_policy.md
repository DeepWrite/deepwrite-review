# Source Policy

No major article may rely only on opinion pieces. Primary sources should be used wherever possible.

## Source Hierarchy

Tier 1 sources include official statistics, court decisions, legislation, government reports, international organizations, peer-reviewed academic literature, and established datasets.

Tier 2 sources include university research centers, major polling organizations, transparent think tank reports, and high-quality newspapers or magazines.

Tier 3 sources include expert commentary, interviews, newsletters, and opinion essays.

Tier 4 sources include social media, partisan blogs, viral posts, and unsourced claims. Tier 4 sources may be used only as evidence of discourse, not as evidence of fact.

## Citation Rule

Every contested factual claim must be traceable to a source. If a claim cannot be verified, mark it as unverified or remove it.

## Quotations

Quotations require source, speaker, date, publication venue, and link or archival reference. Do not invent quotations.

## Korean Source Rule

Republic of Korea coverage should use Korean raw sources wherever possible: official statistics, statutes, court and Constitutional Court decisions, National Assembly records, government reports, policy documents, Bank of Korea publications, regulatory filings, public datasets, National Election Commission data, official speeches, transcripts, and institutional press releases used for event chronology.

Korean-language columns, opinion essays, critical essays, already-interpreted newspaper articles, and magazine-style commentary are not raw sources for DeepWrite Review's own analysis. They may be used only as evidence of discourse, agenda-setting, media attention, or the existence of a public controversy.

When Korean raw sources are used, the article's analytical tools and prose style should still follow the magazine's English-language intellectual review standard: comparative, institutionally precise, evidence-aware, and restrained.
